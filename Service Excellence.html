Service Excellence

The importance of service excellence has increased over the last 10-15 years due to the increase in customer connections provided by the Internet and rapidly evolving technologies. The lines are blurred and each must provide a "service" to attract new customers and most importantly maintain an existing franchise throughout its life. In many organizations, the spirit of service is reflected in all aspects of internal and external engagements - from the first greeting emails to face-to-face meetings and contracts. Putting the customer first is an attitude that can be learned, embedded in systems and processes, and refined in practice over time.
Official research has shown anecdotal data that "service" is one of the most popular reasons for customer dissatisfaction. On the other hand, it also stands out as the biggest single motivation when interviewing clients with long-term relationships and analyzing the data obtained. Therefore, a well-rooted service focus at all levels of the organization is extremely important, because hidden benefits are aligned with commercial benefits, even when environmental or market conditions have made customers look for alternative service providers, especially as an opportunity. to save costs. Looking at the evidence of customer relationship management (CRM) campaigns, wisdom shows that acquiring a new customer costs 4 times more than keeping an existing customer.
